Dr. Mahesh Sharma to inaugurate an extensive exhibition on Indian Civilisation at National Museum, New Delhi
On May 5,2018 An Exhibition called ‘India and the World: A History in Nine Stories’ will be inaugurated at the National Museum auditorium, New Delhi by Dr. Mahesh Sharma ,Minister of State for Culture(I/c) and Minister of State for Environment, Forest and Climate Change. This effort made by the British Museum, London; National Museum, New Delhi; and ChhatrapatiShivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (CSMVS), Mumbai with support of the Culture Ministry.
i.The exhibition evaluates the development of Indian civilization in a sequel order through delicate artifact from India and abroad.
ii. ‘India and the World: A History in Nine Stories’deeps into two million years of history. This exhibition will be a part of memorial of 70 years of India’s independence and a year of major cultural exchange between India and the UK (UK/India 2017).
Highlights of the ‘India and the World: A History in Nine Stories’
i. This exhibition covers 200 objects and works of art from the collections of the British Museum, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Vastu Sangrahalaya (CSMVS), National Museum,and also around 20 museums and private collections across India.
ii. It will starts with Indus Valley civilization and coming down to the present, the artifacts range from stone sculptures, terracotta, coins, tools, inscriptions and manuscripts to textiles, jewellery, Mughal miniatures and contemporary paintings, demonstrating the common threads of human history.
iii.The exhibition is spread over nine sections, each representing a pivotal moment in history: Shared Beginnings (1,700,000 years ago to 2000 BC), First Cities (3000-1000 BC), Empire (600 BC – AD 200), State and Faith (AD 100-750), Picturing the Divine (AD 200-1500), Indian Ocean Traders (AD 200-1650), Court Cultures (AD 1500-1800), Quest for Freedom (1800-Present), and Time Unbound.
iv. It is supported by the Ministry of Culture, Tata Trusts, the Getty Foundation and the Newton Bhabha Fund. It will run for two months till June 30 and is available for public viewing on all days from 10 am to 6 pm, except on Mondays and public holidays.

i. Indus dolphin is one of the world’s rarest mammals found only in India and Pakistan. The first organized census on Indus dolphins are conducting by the Punjab government along with WWF-India.
ii. The Indus dolphins are restricted to only a 185 km stretch between Talwara and Harike Barrage in India’s Beas River in Punjab.
iii. The five day exercise has been conducted to estimate the population of Indus Dolphin by Department of Forests and Wildlife Preservation, Punjab and WWF-India are currently working in two teams.
iv. The population of the Indus dolphin, platanista gangetica minor, numbers are estimated to be around 1,800 across Pakistan in the Indus river. In India, a tiny population survives in this small stretch of the Beas river.

Dakota DC-3 VP 905 aircraft joins IAF again
On 4th May 2018, Dakota DC-3 VP 905 aircraft joined the Indian Air Force again, in an induction ceremony at Hindan air force station, Uttar Pradesh, over four decades after the aircraft fleet was phased out.

NASA launches InSight spacecraft to Mars to study Red Planet’s quakes
On 5th May 2018, NASA launched InSight spacecraft aboard an Atlas V rocket to study “Marsquakes”, from Vandenberg Air Force Base in California, United States.

InSight:
i. InSight has been designed to stay on the surface of Mars and listen to “Marsquakes”. The project costs $993 million.
ii. The US spent $813.8 million on the spacecraft and rocket launch. France and Germany spent $180 million on the instruments.
iii. A pair of mini-spacecraft named Mars Cube One, or MarCO are also launching on the rocket. It cost NASA $18.5 million.
iv. The MarCO are briefcase-sized satellites. They will fly on their own path to Mars behind InSight. They will test tiny new deep space communications equipment.
v. InSight aims to explore interior conditions on Mars, inform efforts to send human explorers to Mars, find out how rocky planets like Earth formed, etc.
vi. InSight is expected to settle on Mars on 26th November 2018. InSight is the short form for Interior Exploration using Seismic Investigations, Geodesy and Heat Transport.
vii. InSight features a seismometer called the Seismic Experiment for Interior Structure. It has been developed by the French Space Agency.
viii. After the lander settles down on the surface of Mars, a robotic arm will place the seismometer on the ground. The lander weighs 794 pound. It has been built by Lockheed Martin Corp.
ix. Another major instrument is a self-hammering probe. It will monitor the flow of heat on Mars’ subsurface.
x. This is named the Heat Flow and Physical Properties Package. It was developed by the German Space Agency along with Polish Space Agency.
xi. The probe will bore down 10 to 16 feet into the subsurface to measure heat from the interior. This will be 15 times deeper than any previous Mars mission.
xii. The solar and battery-powered lander has been designed to operate for 26 Earth months that is one year on Mars. It is expected to observe nearly 100 quakes in that period.

‘Tapanuli Orangutan’, World’s rarest ape on the edge of extinction
Scientists have said that, Tapanuli Orangutan, the rarest ape species on Earth, is on the verge of extinction.
Tapanuli Orangutan:
i. Less than 800 members of the Tapanuli Orangutan species are alive. They are in danger due to mega-projects, deforestation, road building, and poaching. This was mentioned in a study published in the journal Current Biology.
ii. They would not survive unless immediate steps are taken to rescue them. This species was discovered in 2017 in Sumatra, Indonesia.
iii. A major threat to them is a planned $1.6 billion mega-dam project to be constructed by a Chinese state-owned corporation.
