“cVIGIL” : app to enable citizens to report on violation of election code of conduct launched by Election commission of India
On July 3, 2018, The Chief Election Commissioner Shri. O.P. Rawat, along with the Election Commissioners Shri Sunil Arora and Shri Ashok Lavasalaunched a mobile app, called ‘cVIGIL’.

Objective:
Aimed at citizens to report any violation of the model code of conduct during elections.
About the App:
i. The app requires an Android smartphone equipped with a camera, good internet connection and GPS access. The operating system should beAndroid Jellybean and above. The application supports all the latest Android smartphones.
ii. The report of violations of Model Code of Conduct (MCC) that comes into effect from the date of announcement of elections and goes on till a day after the polls.
iii. To prevent misuse the app will not allow uploading of the pre-recorded or old images and videos
iv. The app will not facilitate saving of the photos or videos recorded using the ‘cVIGIL’ app into the phone gallery either.
v. It will be active only in States where elections have been announced. The moment a citizen exits an election-bound State, the app will become inactive.
How it works:
i. The vigilant citizen has to click a picture or record a video of upto two minutes’ duration of the scene of violations of the model code which will be uploaded in the app The automated location mapping will be done by the app using the Geographic Information System.
ii. The user will get 5 minutes to report an incident after having clicked a picture or a video.
iii. After its successful submission through the app, the vigilant citizen gets a Unique ID to track and receive the follow up updates on her or his mobile.
iv. The identity of the complainant will be kept confidential.
v. The information beeps in the District Control Room of a Field Unit. A field unit consists of Flying Squads, Static Surveillance Teams, Reserve teams etc. and has GIS-based mobile application called ‘cVIGIL Dispatcher’, which allows the unit to directly reach the location through navigation technology .
vi. If the incident is found correct, the information is sent to the National Grievance Portal of the Election Commission of India for further action and the vigilant citizen is informed.
Use:
i. The application will be made available for general use by all, right from the forthcoming Assembly elections in the States of Chhattisgarh, Madhya Pradesh, Mizoram and Rajasthan.
ii. Thus, it will also serve as a pilot initiative before the next Lok Sabha General Elections.

Paddy MSP raised to Rs.1,750/quintal, in steepest hike since 2012-13
On 4th July 2018, the government increased the minimum support price (MSP) for paddy by a record Rs 200 per quintal amounting it to Rs 1,750 per quintal.
Paddy MSP raised to Rs.1,750/quintal, in steepest hike since 2012-13:
i. This decision was made by the Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) headed by Prime Minister Narendra Modi.
ii. Home Minister Rajnath Singh said that, the cabinet has approved the MSP of 14 kharif (summer-sown) crops for the 2018-19 season.
iii. CACP (Commission for Agricultural Costs and Prices) has calculated production cost of paddy at Rs 1,166 per quintal.
iv. The government has increased the MSP of paddy (common grade) by Rs 200 to Rs 1,750 per quintal for 2018-19 season.
v. The MSP of Grade A variety of paddy has been increased by Rs 160 per quintal to Rs 1,750. The increase in paddy MSP is 50-51 % over its production cost.
v. The increase in the MSP will cost the exchequer over Rs 15,000 crore. The MSP of cotton (medium staple) has been raised to Rs 5,150 from Rs 4,020 and that of cotton (long staple) to Rs 5,450 from 4,320 per quintal.
vi. The CACP has stated the production cost of medium staple cotton as Rs 3,433 per quintal. The MSP increase is 50 % over the cost.
vii. In pulses, tur MSP has been increased to Rs 5,675 per quintal from Rs 5,450. Moong has been increased to Rs 6,975 per quintal from Rs 5,575.
viii. Urad MSP has been increased to Rs 5,600 from Rs 5,400 per quintal. The MSP of tur and urad has been increased by up to 66 % over their production cost.
ix. In oilseeds, MSP of soyabean has been increased to Rs 3,399 per quintal from Rs 3,050. MSP of groundnut (shell) has been increased to Rs 4,890 per quintal from Rs 4,450.
x. Sunflower seed has been increased to Rs 5,388 per quintal from Rs 4,100, Sesamum has been increased to Rs 6,249 per quintal from 5,300 and niger seed to Rs 5,877 per quintal from Rs 4,050.
xi. There is 50 % increase in the MSP of oilseeds over the cost of production.
