Rare, isolated neutron star discovered
Scientists from NASA have discovered a special kind of neutron star outside the Milky Way galaxy for the first time.
Rare, isolated neutron star discovered:
i. Neutron stars are highly dense cores of massive stars that collapse and go through a supernova explosion.
ii. The newly found neutron star was discovered through data from NASA’s Chandra X-ray Observatory and the European Southern Observatory’s Very Large Telescope (VLT) in Chile.
iii. It is a rare variety having both low magnetic field and no stellar companion. The neutron star is located within the remains of a supernova named 1E 0102.2-7219 (E0102) – in the Small Magellanic Cloud, which is 200,000 light years away from Earth.
iv. The observations of E0102 state that the supernova remnant is dominated by a large ring-shaped structure in X-rays.
v. The data collect by NASA states that, it is an isolated neutron star, created in the supernova explosion nearly two millennia ago.
About NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration):
♦ Administrator – Jim Bridenstine
♦ Headquarters – Washington, D.C., U.S.

Researchers discover hidden mountain ranges, valleys under Antarctica
Researchers have found out mountain ranges and three vast, deep sub-glacial valleys hidden under the Antarctica ice.
Hidden mountain ranges, valleys under Antarctica:
i. The discovery has been published in the journal Geophysical Research Letter. This is the first finding observed from ice penetrating radar data collected in Antarctica as part of the European Space Agency PolarGAP project.
ii. Researchers from Northumbria University, UK, have mapped three huge, sub-glacial valleys in West Antarctica, for the first time.
iii. The largest valley is named the Foundation Trough. It is over 350 kilometres long and 35 kilometres wide.
iv. The Patuxent Trough is over 300 kilometres long and over 15 kilometres wide. The Offset Rift Basin is 150 kilometres long and 30 kilometres wide.
