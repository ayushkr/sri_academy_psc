India gets its first biofortified sorghum (jowar) – Parbhani Shakti
On 5th July 2018, India’s first biofortified sorghum (jowar) named Parbhani Shakti containing higher iron and zinc than regular sorghum was formally launched.
Parbhani Shakti:
i. This improved sorghum variety has been developed by International Crops Research Institute for the Semi-Arid-Tropics (ICRISAT). It was released for cultivation by Vasantrao Naik Marathwada Krishi Vidyapeeth (VNMKV), Maharashtra.
ii. The improved variety ICSR 14001 has been released as ‘Parbhani Shakti’ by VNMKV.
iii. Parbhani Shakti is cost-effective. It has been introduced as a sustainable solution to rectify micronutrient deficiency.
iv. On 5th July 2018, an MoU was signed by ICRISAT and VNMKV for large-scale seed production and dissemination.
v. Parbhani Shakti was developed by ICRISAT under HarvestPlus, a sorghum biofortification project.
vi. It was tested as PVK 1009 in Maharashtra and in All India Co-ordinated Sorghum Improvement Project (AICSIP) Trials.
vii. It was launched as a rainy season variety (Kharif). But it can be grown in post-rainy (Rabi) and summer seasons.
viii. The yield levels are greater (>5.0 t ha-1) in post rainy and summer seasons with irrigation.
ix. In summer season, it can tolerate higher temperatures (41°C) at flowering and seed setting but the flowering could be delayed (80 days).

1st meet of task force on e-commerce policy framework chaired by Commerce Secretary Rita Teaotia.
On July 7, 2018, Commerce Secretary Rita Teaotia chaired the first meeting of the task force set up to recommend the framework for a national policy on e-commerce .
Dignitaries Present in the meeting:
i. Various ministries and departments, high-level representatives from the industry bodies, e-commerce companies, telecommunication and IT companies and independent experts.
ii. Senior officers from the ministries of MSME, consumer affairs, electronics and information technology, corporate affairs and department of telecommunications, Competition Commission of India and DGFT.
Key Points:
i. This is done on the decision of a think-tank constituted on the issue under the chairmanship of Commerce and Industry Minister Suresh Prabhu in April 2018.
ii. The issues to be discussed on this meeting will cover areas of : cross-border data flows, taxation, trade facilitation and logistics, consumer confidence, IPR and future tech, FDI and competition.
iii. The task force was further divided into nine sub-groups for preparing recommendations for the national policy on e-commerce by the Commerce ministry.
iv. The suggestions and recommendations emerging from the task force meeting will be submitted to the think tank for consideration by August 2018.
About task force decisions:
Suggestions by the task force would include : taxation on electronic transmission of goods and services including 3D printing, implication of General Anti-Avoidance Rule (GAAR) on e-commerce, behaviour of current market players in e-commerce and anti-competitive practices policy and creating fair balance between online and physical marketplaces.
About the Sub-Groups of the task force:
i. The first sub-group is working on issues including cross border transfer of information by electronic means, data protection and location of computing facilities
ii. The second on digital product, custom duties and taxation
iii. The third on trade facilitation measures and logistics
iv. The fourth is working on measures enhancing consumer confidence.
v. The fifth sub-group focuses on protection of IP and contours of future technology such as AI and block chain,
vi. The sixth on payment systems and financial technologies,
vii. The seventh on interconnection issues and net neutrality,
viii. The seven and eighth are working on skills, education and training and SMEs and FDI and competition issues respectively.

Declaration of entire animal kingdom as ‘legal entities’ after Ganga and Yamuna : Uttarakhand HC.
On July 5,2018, The Uttarakhand high court declared the entire animal kingdom, including birds and aquatic animals, as a legal entity having rights of a “living person”.
This comes a year after the court accorded the status of “living entity” to the Ganga and Yamuna rivers (in March 2017) which was stayed by Supreme Court.
Key Points:
i. This decision was taken by a division bench of justices Rajiv Sharma and Lok Pal Singh.
ii. Invoking Article 21 of the Constitution, the court said that the animals can no longer be treated as properties and will be conferred with the status of “legal entity/legal person”.
iii. Earlier, similar rights were given to corporations, Hindu gods, holy scriptures and rivers.
iv. The court also declared all citizens of Uttarakhand “persons in loco parentis” (in the place of a parent) giving them the responsibility to protect animals and ensure their welfare.
v. The Division Bench also gave directions ranging from the amount of load allowed to be pulled by various animals in accordance with the kind of carriage being pulled to the amount of riders per carriage.
vi. It also directed ban on the use of spike or other sharp tackle or equipment on the animal, that no animal should draw vehicles if the temperature exceeds 37 degree C or drops to 5 degree C.
vii. For animal safety the court also mandated the need for fluorescent reflectors in carriages and animals, certificates of unladen weight of vehicles, compulsory shelter of suitable size for horses, bullocks and stray cattle and a direction to the veterinary doctors of Uttarakhand to treat any stray animals brought to them or by visiting them.
Background:
The order came in response to a public interest litigation seeking directions to restrict the movement of horse carts/tongas between Nepal and India through Champawat district and highlighted that ailing, infirm and old horses were being abandoned by the owners in the Indian territory.

Approval of USD 6.69 billion for 13 peacekeeping operations in 2018-19 by UN member states; first in 40 years.
On July 7, 2018, The UN member states approved a USD 6.69 billion dollar budget for 13 peacekeeping operations from July 1, 2018, to June 30, 2019 and implementation of other reforms for the first time in more than 40 years.
Objective:
In making UN a more accountable, transparent, and efficient organization.
Key Points:
i. This decision was taken on the recommendation of the 5th Committee (Administrative and Budgetary) of the General Assembly .
ii. It is the second year in a row in which the committee has made significant cuts to the overall peacekeeping budget.
Recommendations of the Committee:
i. The contribution of USD 6.69 billion dollars from member states would be invested in UN Peacekeeping missions.
ii. The missions include MINUJUSTH (United Nations Mission for Justice Support in Haiti), MINURSO (Mission for the Referendum in Western Sahara), MINUSCA (Multidimensional Integrated Stabilisation Mission in the Central African Republic), MONUSCO (Stabilisation Mission in the Democratic Republic of the Congo) and UNIFIL (Interim Force in Lebanon).
iii. The committee also approved of major management reforms, including the creation of two new departments focused on political and peace building affairs. They are: Departments of Political and Peace building Affairs and the Department of Peace Operations.
iv. This was announced on a draft resolution titled ‘Special subjects relating to the programme budget for the biennium 2018-2019’.
v. Further, the assembly would create four stand-alone divisions for Africa, effective from January 1 2019.
vi. Approved the “Middle East Division” where the lead responsibility for the United Nations Assistance Mission in Afghanistan (UNAMA) and United Nations Assistance Mission for Iraq (UNAMI) would be placed in the Department of Political and Peacebuilding Affairs.
About the contribution of various member states of UN:
i. The United States is the biggest contributor to the United Nations, paying 22 per cent of the USD 5.4 billion core budget and 28.5 per cent of the USD 7.9 billion peacekeeping budget.
ii. China is the other top contributor with 10.3 per cent and Japan with 9.7 per cent.
iii. Number of Member States: 193.

NASA’s Parker Solar Probe gets ‘revolutionary’ heat shield
NASA’s Parker Solar Probe, the mission that aims to get the closest to the Sun than ever by any human-made object has got its revolutionary heat shield “Thermal Protection System or TPS” permanently attached to the spacecraft.
NASA’s Parker Solar Probe gets Thermal Protection System or TPS:
i. NASA’s Parker Solar Probe will be launched in August 2018. The probe’s heat shield is called the Thermal Protection System or TPS.
ii. It was reinstalled on the spacecraft on 27th June 2018. The re-installation of the heat shield was attached to the spacecraft during testing in 2017.
iii. This mission has been in the making for 60 years. Parker Solar Probe will travel to the Sun’s corona, a region of the solar atmosphere.
iv. The spacecraft’s orbit will carry it to within 4 million miles of the Sun’s surface with the help of its heat shield. There the spacecraft will collect data about the inner workings of the corona.
v. The heat shield’s diameter is 8 feet. It will safeguard everything within its umbra, the shadow it casts on the spacecraft.
v. As Parker Solar Probe approaches closest to the Sun, temperatures on the heat shield will reach around 1,371 degrees Celsius. But the spacecraft and its instruments will be maintained at a relatively comfortable temperature of nearly 29.4 degrees Celsius.
vi. The heat shield consists of 2 panels of superheated carbon-carbon composite sandwiching a lightweight 4.5-inch-thick carbon foam core.
vii. The Sun-facing side of the heat shield is sprayed with a specially formulated white coating to reflect most of the Sun’s energy away from the spacecraft.
viii. Parker Solar Probe will travel at a speed of 69,2018 km per hour at its closest approach to the Sun. The heat shield weighs only 72.5 kg.
About National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA):
♦ Administrator – Jim Bridenstine
♦ Headquarters – Washington, D.C., U.S.

Law Commission recommends regulated and taxable gambling, betting on sports
On 5th July 2018, The Law Commission recommended that gambling and betting on sports, including cricket, to be allowed as regulated activities taxable under the direct and indirect tax regimes and used as a source for attracting foreign direct investment (FDI).
Law Commission recommends regulated and taxable gambling, betting on sports;
i. The commission’s report, Legal Framework: Gambling and Sports Betting including Cricket in India has recommended various changes in the law for regulating betting and generating tax revenues from it.
ii. The Parliament might also enact a model law for regulating gambling that would be adopted by the states or Parliament might legislate in exercise of its powers under Articles 249 or 252 of the Constitution.
iii. If legislation is made under Article 252, states other than the consenting states can freely adopt the same.
iv. The commission has also recommended linking Aadhar or PAN card of an individual indulging in betting and gambling. This would make the transaction cashless and regulate illegal activities like money laundering.
v. The commission has also recommended amending the laws regulating forex and India’s FDI policy to allow investments in the casino and online gaming industry.
