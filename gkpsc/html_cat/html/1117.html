Vodafone-Idea merger gets govt’s conditional approval
On July 10, 2018, The Department of Telecommunications (DoT) has conditionally approved the merger of Vodafone India with Idea Cellular, the largest M&A deal in the sector, which will displace Bharti Airtel from the No. 1 spot after over 15 years.

i. The Merged entity will have a user base of 430 million.
Key Points:
i. The conditions for approval include payment of Rs 7,268 crore which is split between a bank guarantee of Rs 3,342 crore to cover what Ideaowes on account of one-time spectrum charges and cash of Rs 3,926 crore by Vodafone towards the market price for non-auctioned airwaves.
ii. Upon the completion of the payment the department will transfer of Vodafone India’s licences and Vodafone Mobile Services Ltd.’s licences to Idea Cellular.
iii. Among other conditions, Idea needs to replace Vodafone’s bank guarantees worth Rs 6,452 crore held by DoT with its own. This is on account of spectrum payments that fall due over the next one year.
iv. Together, Vodafone Idea will have 37.5% revenue market share and 39% customer market share, making them a stronger entity.
After the merger:
i. Vodafone and the Aditya Birla Group need to collectively inject at least Rs 15,000 crore of fresh capital into the combined entity to boost the merged entity’s 4G network capacity and bring it on comparable levels with Jio and Airtel.
ii. Idea has already raised over Rs 10,000 crore from the sale of its captive towers, promoter equity infusion and private placement of share.
iii. Vodafone will infuse over Rs 7,000 crore.
iv. Idea recently got approval to raise another Rs 15,000 crore via non-convertible debentures.
v. Vodafone Idea will need to reduce revenue market share in Kerala, Maharashtra and Gujarat to less than 50% within a year of the final approval.
vi. Kumar Mangalam Birla, chairman of the $44.3-billion Aditya Birla Group, will be the non-executive chairman.

World’s oldest colours discovered by scientists from The Australian National University in Sahara desert in Africa
On July 10, 2018, Scientists have discovered the oldest colors in the geological record, 1.1-billion-year-old bright pink pigments extracted from rocks deep beneath the Sahara desert in Africa.
i. This was done by the team from The Australian National University (ANU).
The fossils range from blood red to deep purple in their concentrated form, and bright pink when diluted.
ii. The bright pink pigments are the molecular fossils of chlorophyll that were produced by ancient photosynthetic organisms inhabiting an ancient ocean that has long since vanished.
iii. The researchers crushed the billion-year-old rocks to powder before extracting and analysing molecules of ancient organisms from them.
iv. The research has published in the journal- Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences.
