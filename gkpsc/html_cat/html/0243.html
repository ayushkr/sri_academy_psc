National News

1.NCERT syllabus to be reduced to half from 2019.

  • Prakash Javadekar, Union Human Resources Development Minister, said NCERT syllabus will be reduced to half from the 2019 academic session.
  • This move is aimed at ensuring that students get time to pursue other activities for their all-round development.

2.Marriage is not a contract for legal sexual pleasure: Delhi Court.

  • A family court in Delhi recently dismissed a man’s divorce plea, saying he cannot take advantage of his own wrongs, as he committed extreme acts of sexual abuse and domestic violence on his wife.
  • The court observed that sexual intimacy is a key component in a lawful marriage, but marriage is not a contract for legal sexual gratification.

3.More emphasis on waste to wealth and waste to energy: PM Modi.

  • PM Modi said that this year’s budget emphasizes on waste to wealth and waste to energy using biogas for villages during ‘Mann ki Baat’.
  • He also praised Chhattisgarh’s ‘garbage festival’ which aimed to encourage creative use of garbage.

4.IndiGo to shift flights to Delhi airport’s T2 after SC order.

  • Complying to the order of SC, IndiGo will shift some of its flights from Delhi airport’s Terminal 1 to Terminal 2 in the coming weeks.
  • The statement came after the apex court rejected IndiGo’s plea challenging a Delhi Court order.

International News

1.N Korea is willing to talk to US: S Korea.

  • S Korea said that N Korea is willing to talk to the US amid the ongoing tensions between the two nations.
  • This comes after the US announced the ‘largest-ever’ sanctions on N Korea over its nuclear and ballistic missile programme.

2.Philippine President demands raise in salary.

  • Arguing that his salary was not enough, Philippine President Rodrigo Duterte said that he should be paid more because he has two wives.
  • Adding that he gets tired by ‘flying and saluting’, Duterte said, ‘You know how much I earn?…My ideal salary, if would estimate it, I should be getting one million, five hundred.’

3.30-day ceasefire in Syria after 500 people killed last week.

  • The United Nations Security Council unanimously approved a 30-day ceasefire resolution in Syria to allow for humanitarian aid deliveries and medical evacuations.
  • The resolution comes after week-long airstrikes and bombings that killed over 500 people in the country’s Eastern Ghouta enclave.

4.Children forced to rape for survival in South Sudan war: UN.

  • The UN Human Rights Commission has said that the children in South Sudan are forced to rape their relatives as well as watch them murdered in order to remain alive.
  • The commission added that civilians has had their eyes gouged out, throats slit or were castrated.

Business News
1.SBI has the highest amount of wilful defaulters.

  • India’s largest lender SBI has the highest amount of wilful defaults amounting to Rs.27,716 crore till December 2017.
  • PNB has wilful defaults of Rs.12,754 crore while Bank of India has 314 wilful defaulters who owe Rs.6,104 crore to the bank.

2.Arun Jaitley rules out privatisation of state-owned banks.

  • Finance Minister Arun Jaitley has ruled out privatisation of public sector banks as a response to the PNB fraud.
  • He said, “It is very challenging to privatise state-run banks as it requires an amendment to the law and involves large political consensus.”

3.Banks told to link SWIFT with CBS after PNB fraud.

  • RBI has asked banks to integrate SWIFT interbank messaging system with Core Banking Solution(CBS) by April 30.
  • CBS is a software that supports the daily transactions and accounts within a bank internally. SWIFT network is used by financial institutions worldwide to securely transmit information and instructions using a system of codes.

4.Bank frauds affect Ease of Doing Business: Jaitley.

  • Finance Minister Arun Jaitley has said that regular occurrence of bank frauds and wilful defaults hamper the efforts for improving ease of doing business.
  • He also said that the multiple layers of auditors did a casual job or chose to look the other way, referring to the PNB fraud.

3.B Kumar records most wickets by an Indian pacer in a T20I series.

  • Bhuvaneshwar Kumar took 7 wickets in the three T20I against S Africa to set record for the most wickets by an Indian pacer in a bilateral T20I series.
  • Kumar had an average of 9.57 and economy rate of 6.09 in the series.
