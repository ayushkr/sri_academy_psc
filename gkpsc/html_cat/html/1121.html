Swachh Survekshan Grameen, 2018: a nationwide survey of rural India launched by the Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation
On July 14, 2018, Secretary, Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation, Shri Parameswaran Iyer, launched ‘Swachh Survekshan Grameen’ 2018, a survey of rural cleanliness programme, to rank all districts and states on the basis of qualitative and quantitative evaluation.

i. Top performing states and districts will be awarded on October 2.
ii. An independent survey agency will conduct the survey in all districts from 1st to 31st August 2018.
Objective:
To undertake a ranking of states and districts on the basis of their performance attained on key quantitative and qualitative SBM-G parameters.
Key Points:
About the Survey:
i. The ranking will be based on set of cleanliness parameters, survey of public places, citizens’ perception of “swachhta” (cleanliness) and their recommendations for the improvement of the programme, and data from the ‘Swachh Bharat Mission’ in rural areas.
ii. It will be based on district level surveys of public places like schools, anganwadis, haat-bazaars, panchayat and citizen’s perception of ‘Swachhta’.
iii. The Swachh Survekshan Gramin plan will cover, 6,800 villages in 680 districts across the country and 34,000 public places.
iv. Around 54,000 citizens will be interviewed for their feedback on Swachh Bharat Mission.
v. The ranking would be done giving 65% weightage to outcome from the survey and 35% to the service level parameters to be obtained from the IMIS of the ministry.
Other Announcements in the Launch:
i. The audio-visual communication campaign, featuring prominent celebrities including Swachh Bharat ambassadors including Shri Amitabh Bachchan and Shri Sachin Tendulkar was also launched.
ii. The Swachh Survekshan Gramin (SSG) 2018 logo and the SSG 2018 brochure detailing essential facts about the initiative was also released.
Achievements:
i. According to the progress figures of SBM(G) in rural India, over 7.7 crore toilets have been built in rural India with a usage of 93% as per an independent third-party survey conducted in 2017-18 across all States/UTs.
ii. Nearly 4 lakh villages, over 400 districts and 19 States and Union Territories have declared themselves free from open defecation.

World famous Rath Yatra being celebrated in Puri, Odisha
Every year in June or July the Ratha Yatra, the Festival of Chariot: Chariots of Lord Jagannath is celebrated every year at Puri in Odisha.It is considered as India and world oldest Ratha Yatra.

i. It will be celebrated on the second day of shukla pakshya (waxing cycle of moon) of Ashadh Maas (3rd month in Lunar Calendar).
Reason behind this festival:-
i. The festival describes Jagannath’s annual visit to Gundicha Temple via Mausi Maa Temple (aunt’s home) near Balagandi Chaka, Puri. The deity of Jagannath, his elder brother Balabhadra and younger sister Subhadra are taken out in a procession to Gundicha Temple and remain there for nine days.
ii. The return journey of Puri Jagannath Ratha Yatra is known as Bahuda Yatra.

New radio telescope unveiled by South Africa for clearer image of distant galaxies
On July 13, 2018, South Africa has unveiled a powerful radio telescope, part of an international project that scientists say will provide new details on the Milky Way and other galaxies.

i. They are releasing the “clearest view yet” of the centre of our galaxy through this telescope.
ii. It is known as the 64-antenna MeerKAT, which was completed after a decade of design and construction.
iii. The Square Kilometere Array or SKA telescope had support from various countries like Canada, China, India, Italy, Britain, Spain, Sweden, the Netherlands and New Zealand.
iv. It has a radio telescope site in Australia as well as in South Africa.
About MeerKAT:
i. MeerKAT, originally the Karoo Array Telescope, is a radio telescope consisting of 64 mirrors.
ii. It the largest and most sensitive radio telescope in the southern hemisphere until the Square Kilometer Array is completed in approximately 2024.
About Square Kilometer Array:
The Square Kilometre Array (SKA) is a large multi radio telescope project aimed to be built in Australia and South Africa. If built, it would have a total collecting area of approximately one square kilometer.

SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY

First-ever 3-D colour X-ray on a human performed by 2 New Zealand Scientists
On July 13,2018, the first-ever 3-D, colour X-ray on a human was done by 2 New Zealand scientists named Phil and Anthony Butler (father and son).
This was done using CERN’s imaging technology called Medipix in its lab.
i. This consisted of using a technique that improves the field of medical diagnostics.
ii. The new device, based on the traditional black-and-white X-ray, incorporates particle-tracking technology of CERN.
iii. It works like a camera detecting and counting individual sub-atomic particles as they collide with pixels while its shutter is open. This allows for high-resolution, high-contrast pictures.
iv. According to the CERN, the images very clearly show the difference between bone, muscle and cartilage and the position and size of cancerous tumours.
v. The technology is being commercialised by New Zealand, company MARS Bioimaging, linked to the universities of Otago and Canterbury which helped develop it.

Nilgiri tahr threatened by Climate change: Ecological Engineering
On July 11, 2018, international journal Ecological Engineering published that climate change is threatening the Nilgiri tahr.
i. According to the study, 60% of the population of the goat could be lost from 2030.
ii. The population in Chinnar, Eravikulam and Parambikulam is still strong enough to withstand the climate change but those of Tamil Nadu’s Kalakkad tiger reserve and wildlife sanctuaries if Peppara, Neyyar etc could experience habitat loss.
iii. The study puts emphasis on conservation measure and management for the habitat.
About Nilgiri Tahrs:
♦ They are the state animal of Tamil Nadu .
♦ As of now, only around 2,500 Nilgiri Tahrs are left in the wild.
♦ They are found only in the high altitudes of Western ghats.
