Scientists have discovered the world’s oldest color bright pink

  • The color of bubble gum, flamingos and cotton candy — bright pink — is the world’s oldest color, according to a recent study.
  • Researchers discovered the ancient pink pigments in 1.1-billion-year-old rocks deep beneath the Sahara Desert in the Taoudeni Basin of Mauritania, West Africa, making them the oldest colors in the geological record.

CURRENT AFFAIRS: INTERNATIONAL

Satyarup Siddhanta becomes second Indian to climb world’s highest volcano

  • Satyarup Siddhanta has become the second Indian mountaineer after Malli Mastan Babu to climb Mt Ojos del Salado, the highest volcano in the world. Satyarup is also set to be the first Indian to climb Mt Sidley, the highest volcano of Antarctica. Mount Ojos del Salado is a stratovolcano (a volcano built up of alternate layers of lava and ash) in the Andes on the Argentina-Chile border and the highest active volcano in the world at 6,893 metres (22,615 ft).
