Mongolia launches construction of its first oil refinery with $1 billion Indian aid
On June 23, 2018, Mongolia launched construction of its first oil refinery that is funded by India in a ceremony that was attended by Minister of Home Affairs Rajnath Singh and the Mongolian Prime Minister Khurelsukh Ukhnaa.
Key points:
i. It will be capable of processing 1.5 million tonnes of crude oil each year That is about 30,000 barrels per day (bpd).
ii. The cost of the refinery is estimated at $1.35 billion, and it will include a pipeline and its own power plant.
iii. It is planned for completion in late 2022.
iv. It will meet demand for gasoline, diesel, aviation fuel and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG).
v. The project is expected to boost Mongolia’s gross domestic product by 10 per cent.
Background:
i. Mongolia imported almost 1.5 million tonnes of oil products last year.
ii. Mongolia, a large landlocked country wedged between giants China and Russia, has a population of just 3 million.
iii. The refinery’s financing is part of a $1-billion credit line agreement between Mongolia and the Export-Import Bank of India, made during a 2015 visit by Prime Minister Narendra Modi.

Six new spider species named after Enid Blyton characters discovered in Sri Lanka
On June 23, 2018, Six new species of minute goblin spiders discovered in the forests of Sri Lanka have been named after fictional characters described by English children’s writer Enid Blyton.
i. The names given were: The goblins Bom, Snooky and Tumpy and the brownies Chippy, Snippy and Tiggy .
ii. These are taken from ‘The Goblins Looking-Glass’ (1947), ‘Billy’s Little Boats’ (1971) and ‘The Firework Goblins’ (1971).
iii. A total of nine goblin spider species in six genera was described by Scientists Suresh P Benjamin and Sasanka Ranasinghe of the National Institute of Fundamental Studies in Sri Lanka.

International Olympic Day celebrated worldwide in June 23
On June 23, 2018, The International Olympic Day is celebrated all around the world for commemorating the birth of modern Olympic games on June 23, 1894 at Sorbonne in Paris.

i. Olympic day was introduced in 1948.
ii.Olympic Day Run is an international Olympic Movement activity promoting mass participation of sports held in June organized by National Olympic Committees (NOCs).

United Nations Public Service Day – 23 June
The United Nations Public Service Day is celebrated of every year on June 23.

i. The UN Public Service Day celebrates

  • Value and virtue of public service to the community;
  • Highlights the contribution of public service in the development process;
  • Recognizes the work of public servants, and encourages young people to pursue careers in the public sector.

ii. The 2018 United Nations Public Service Forum will take place in Marrakesh, in the Kingdom of Morocco from 21- 23 June 2018, with the theme of “Transforming governance to realize the Sustainable Development Goals”.

International Widows Day – June 23
International Widows Day is a global awareness day on 23rd June every year. This Day was introduced to address poverty and injustice faced by widows and their children in many countries.
i. The day was launched by the United Nations in 2010 to raise awareness of the violation of human rights that widows suffer in many countries following the death of their spouses.
ii. The ultimate goal of the day is to develop resources and policy to empower widows and allow them to have access to education, work, healthcare and lives free of violence and abuse.
